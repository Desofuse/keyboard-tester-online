Keyboard layouts, explained

The keyboard tester highlights keys based on the physical key position (event.code), while your operating system also maps a character (event.key) based on the selected language/layout. If you see a “mismatch”, it usually means your layout is different from the printed keycaps.

QWERTY vs AZERTY vs QWERTZ

  • QWERTY is common in the US and many countries.
  • AZERTY is common in France and Belgium.
  • QWERTZ is common in Germany and parts of Central Europe.

Tip: In the tester, compare event.code (physical key) vs event.key (typed character).

ANSI vs ISO (physical keyboard shape)

  • ANSI (common in the US) has a wide horizontal Enter key and usually no extra key near left Shift.
  • ISO (common in Europe) has a tall Enter key and often an extra key next to left Shift.

How to check your layout

  1. Open the tester and press a few letter keys (A, Q, Z).
  2. If the physical position highlights correctly but the character is “wrong”, switch your OS layout.
  3. On Windows: Settings → Time & Language → Language & region → Keyboard.
  4. On macOS: System Settings → Keyboard → Input Sources.
